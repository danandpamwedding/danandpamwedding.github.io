About Us

Pamela Chan

Pam was born on June 11th, 1981 in one of the greatest cities in the world... San Francisco, CA! From an early start, she grew up loving every bit of city life...the food, the culture, the sports teams (Go Niners!!! Go Giants!!!),... and the food.

But despite all the wonderful things that the city had to offer her, nothing was more important to her than being surrounded by the love and support of her family, her very large extended family, and friends. Even to this day, she finds herself reminiscing of all the fond memories and family gatherings that filled her childhood/adolescent years. It was during these years that she would meet her maid of honor and best friend, Mari. In 1999 when she graduated from St. Ignatius College Prep, Pam said a very bittersweet good-bye to the city and family and friends she loved and headed south for the gates of Westwood.

Arriving at UCLA (Go Bruins!!!), Pam was ready to begin the next big adventure. At first it was very difficult adjusting to her new surroundings even though she was just a short drive away from the guidance of her older sister, Jen. She even questioned if she could ever love any other city than the city by the bay. At that time, she had planned to major in the sciences, make 4 years of unforgettable memories, and then move back to the bay where she belonged... or so she thought...

Well, she had one of the three right. UCLA pushed Pam to work hard academically as she majored in International Development Studies and Asian American Studies. It was also on the beautiful UCLA campus that her friendship with Mari would continue to grow as they supported each other through the years and through the many ups and downs of life, love and whiskey. UCLA was also the place where Pam would meet dancing queen and bridesmaid Ruby. Although Ruby transferred after 2 years, it was the love of music, dancing and overall fun that kept her and Pam's friendship strong.

During college, many of Pam's friends urged her to pledge Delta Sigma Pi where she first met bridesmaid Cindy and thus, an unforeseeable friendship began. Following college, the two became closer friends and encouraged each other professionally as each one was preparing for graduate school. As both girls worked hard towards their profession, they also needed some time to play... and that's how bridesmaid Brenda entered Pam's life. The ever-so-friendly and genuine Brenda always kept a positive outlook on life and made Pam want to see the world in the same way. With her friends by her side and her sister Jen just a short drive away, life in LA couldn't get any better. And then one day, she met Nishi...

Daniel Nishimura

Dan was born on May 10th, 1979 on a small island in the Pacific Ocean called Oahu. Besides school and family life, Dan spent a significant portion of his early formable years playing video games. Clint, Dan's best man, also had a strong affinity for video games which helped start a lifelong friendship and that friendship became more like family.

Dan moved to a different school for 8th grade and high school, but still kept in touch with Clint. At the new high school, Dan donned the nickname "Nishi" and that's where he met groomsmen Matt and Prid. During high school, he discovered a love for the ocean waves and surfed whenever he had the opportunity. Matt was the surfing buddy of choice, and Prid, well, didn't have the same passion for the ocean, but Nishi very much enjoyed Prid's presence on land.

After high school, Dan decided to stay home for college even though most of his classmates and friends ventured to the mainland. Unsure of what he was going to do with his life, having written his first C program in college sparked an interest he's never felt and started his software engineering path. Although Dan enjoyed his computer science courses, he couldn't find anyone he could connect with. Luckily one day Dan, wearing his usual boardshorts and slippers, met groomsman Mark, wearing his usual baggy jeans and k-pop star haircut, during one of their class exams. An unlikely pair, the two became great friends and pushed each other academically, professionally, and convivially.

Soon after college graduation, Dan felt the need to explore life outside of Hawaii. He hung up his surfboard and moved to LA to advance his studies in graduate school and to truly begin his career. Groomsman Kelly graciously hopped into Dan's life, first as a colleague but now as trusted friend whom Dan could always rely on. However, before meeting Kelly, Dan met the most important and only person he'd want to spend the rest of his life with... Pam.

Pam + Dan

One Friday night, Dan decided to tag along with his coworkers to a LA club. That same night, Pam was out playing the wing-woman to help her friend meet a girl. Fate then brought Pam and Dan together that night.

Dan's version of that night:

Dan was attracted to Pam's addictive smile and Pam couldn't resist the island boy charm. With his smooth moves and flowing hair, he lured Pam on to the dance floor where they made their first and everlasting connection. Dan lost her later that night, but knew he'd taken his place in her mind and knew he would eventually see her again.

Pam's version of that night:

So Pam's college friend, Debbie, introduced her to a coworker, Dan. That's pretty much it.

Pam and Dan saw each other often at different gatherings and became good friends. Both were foodies and had similar sarcastic humor. Dan knew there was something special about Pam. It was something about her cute demeanor and big heart that attracted him to her. Conversely, it took Pam a while to open her heart to Dan. However, she found herself comparing all the guys that crossed her path to Dan. Every outing was a fun time when the two were together. It was inevitable that the two were a match made in the Jack in the Box drive-through. That's where the 3 words would be said and thus began their journey as a couple.

The Proposal

After dating for over 4 years, they felt the marriage pressure from both family and friends. Pam was soon feeling the pressure from the mothers to plan a wedding when they weren't even engaged. Little did she know, Dan had a secret plan. Months prior, Dan and Pam planned a trip to NYC to attend a cousin's wedding and to visit Mari. Mari had offered to take photos of them around the city. With Mari's help, Dan devised a plan to finally pop the question. He intentionally asked Mari to have the last photo shoot in Central Park at a private area near the pond. When the moment was ready, Mari gave the cue and asked, "Is there anything else you want to do?". Dan then grabbed the ring, went on one knee, and asked her to marry him. With tears of joy and surprise in her eyes, she jumped up, grabbed him and said, "Yes!"

Places to Eat

Philz Coffee: One of Dan and Pam's favorite coffee places anywhere. Each cup of coffee is individually hand brewed. A definite must try! Breakfast burritos and pastries are yummy too.

www.philzcoffee.com

Ferry Building: Filled with various artisanal shops and restaurants. Personal favorites are Hog Island Oyster Co. and Blue Bottle Coffee. They also have an amazing farmer's market on Tuesdays, Thursdays, and Saturdays, featuring vendors such as Roli Roti, known for their porchetta sandwiches.

www.ferrybuildingmarketplace.com

Plouf: Tucked away in a small alley way in the financial district, you'll find Plouf along with other delicious restaurants. This is one of our favorite places to go for a pot of mussels and clams with a glass of wine. Pomme frites are to die for as well.

www.ploufsf.com

Tartine Bakery: In the mission district you'll find this bakery and cafe. They're known for their pastries, breads, and sandwiches.

www.tartinebakery.com

Hong Kong Lounge I or II: On our last trip here, we were able to experience amazing dim sum, quite possibly the best we've had. We recommend everything, but in particular the baked BBQ pork buns. Find them on Yelp for more info.

Bi-Rite Creamery: Handcrafted ice cream and baked goods. They also have a market with deliciously prepared foods.

www.biritecreamery.com

Mitchell's Ice Cream: Another SF ice cream institution with unexpected flavors.

www.mitchellsicecream.com

Off the Grid: For you food truck lovers, you'll find all the popular food trucks in one place. If you can't make it out there, check out their website for links to each truck. We recommend "The Chairman" for yummy baos.

offthegridsf.com

Golden Gate Bakery: Best egg tarts ever. They also server other delicious chinese baked goods. Find them on yelp for more info.
